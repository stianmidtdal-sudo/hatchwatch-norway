How it works

Hatch Watch combines two independent prediction models and picks the winner based on how far out you're looking. Close to the event, real water temperature data extrapolated to hatch threshold wins. Farther out, a Growing Degree Day (GDD) model takes over — accumulating heat from January 1st, crossing a species-specific thermal threshold when the bugs say "it's time."

Ice-out dates are predicted using Stefan's law — a physical ice growth/melt model calibrated against satellite-observed ice-out records from each location. Snow cover, river hydrology, and air temperature from nearby MET stations all feed in.

It's either very clever, or deeply unnecessary. Possibly both.
